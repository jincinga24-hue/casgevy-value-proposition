### Research Brief | Speaker: Henry | ~3.5 minutes

## WHAT MAKES IT UNIQUE

### 1. World's First Approved CRISPR Therapy

Casgevy (exagamglogene autotemcel) received the world's first CRISPR gene therapy approval — first from the UK MHRA on 16 November 2023, then the US FDA on 8 December 2023 for sickle cell disease (SCD), followed by a second FDA approval for transfusion-dependent beta-thalassaemia (TDT) on 16 January 2024 (FDA, 2023; FDA, 2024; MHRA, 2023).

---

### 2. One-Time Treatment, Potentially Curative

**Phase 3 CLIMB-SCD-121 trial** (SCD, n=46 treated, 30 evaluable at primary analysis):

- **96.7%** of patients (29/30) were free of severe vaso-occlusive crises for ≥12 consecutive months
- **100%** were free of hospitalisation for severe VOCs for the same period
- Median follow-up: 19.3 months; longest follow-up: >5 years

> Frangoul et al. (2024), *New England Journal of Medicine*, 390(18), 1649–1662. doi: 10.1056/NEJMoa2309676

**Phase 3 CLIMB-THAL-111 trial** (TDT, n=52 treated, 35 evaluable):

- **91.4%** achieved transfusion independence for ≥12 months
- Mean haemoglobin achieved: 13.1 g/dL

> Locatelli et al. (2024), *New England Journal of Medicine*, 390(18), 1663–1676. doi: 10.1056/NEJMoa2309673

**Long-term data (ASH Annual Meeting, 2024):** At median 33.2-month follow-up, 93% of SCD patients remained VOC-free and 94.2% of TDT patients remained transfusion-independent. Editing levels were stable in bone marrow over time, confirming durable long-term stem cell editing.

> Vertex Pharmaceuticals (2024); *Blood*, 146(Suppl 1):2537

---

### 3. No Donor Required — Autologous Approach

Casgevy uses the patient's own haematopoietic stem cells, edited ex vivo via electroporation of CRISPR/Cas9 ribonucleoprotein (RNP). No viral vector is used. There is no random genomic integration and no foreign DNA remains in the cells after editing. This eliminates the need for a donor match, graft-versus-host disease risk, and immune rejection entirely.

> FDA Package Insert — CASGEVY (2023); casgevyhcp.com, Mechanism of Action

## VS. COMPETITORS

### Casgevy vs. Lyfgenia (bluebird bio)

| Feature | Casgevy | Lyfgenia |
|---|---|---|
| Mechanism | CRISPR/Cas9 (non-integrating) | Lentiviral vector (random integration) |
| US List Price | $2.2 million | $3.1 million |
| Indications | SCD + TDT | SCD only |
| Black box warning | None | **Yes — haematologic malignancy (AML/MDS)** |
| VOC-free rate (trial) | 96.7% | 88% (28/32 evaluable) |
| Manufacturer status | Vertex (~$120B market cap, 2025) | Acquired for ~$29M after near-bankruptcy |

Lyfgenia carries an FDA black box warning after two patients in earlier trials developed acute myeloid leukaemia (AML) and a third developed myelodysplastic syndrome (MDS). This arose from lentiviral insertional mutagenesis: the vector integrates semi-randomly near proto-oncogenes, and viral LTR enhancer sequences can drive their aberrant over-expression. Casgevy's CRISPR approach makes a single, defined, targeted disruption at a pre-specified genomic locus and leaves no foreign DNA behind — structurally eliminating this risk mechanism. No off-target edits were observed in CLIMB trial clinical data.

> FDA Package Insert — LYFGENIA (2023); FDA Summary Basis for Regulatory Action — CASGEVY (January 2024); Hacein-Bey-Abina et al., *Journal of Clinical Investigation* (2008)

bluebird bio, Lyfgenia's manufacturer, collapsed financially and was acquired by Carlyle Group and SK Capital for approximately $29 million — down from a peak market capitalisation of over $11 billion — after warning investors it lacked sufficient cash to survive past Q1 2025.

> BioPharma Dive (2025); Fierce Pharma (May 2025)

### Casgevy vs. Standard of Care

| Treatment | Annual Cost | Curative? | Key Limitation |
|---|---|---|---|
| Hydroxyurea | ~$16,810/year | No | ~50% VOC reduction only; lifelong daily dosing required |
| Chronic transfusions + iron chelation | ~$160,000–$220,000/year | No | Lifelong; causes iron overload; alloimmunisation risk |
| Bone marrow transplant | ~$150,000–$250,000 (one-time) | Potentially | <20% of SCD patients have a matched sibling donor; ~14% GvHD rate |
| Casgevy | $2.2M (one-time) | Potentially | Complex ~6-month manufacturing process; conditioning chemotherapy required |

> Moore et al. / MSH Investigators, *American Journal of Hematology* (2000); ASH *Blood* (2016); PMC4581443; NBK538515

When decades of hospitalisation, chronic transfusion, chelation therapy, and quality-of-life costs are modelled, Casgevy's long-term economic case strengthens considerably against lower-cost alternatives.

---

### Pipeline Competitors

The competitive landscape has narrowed substantially. Graphite Bio, Sangamo/Sanofi, and the Intellia/Novartis programme all discontinued their SCD gene therapy programs by 2023–2024.

> BioPharma Dive (2023)

The only credible near-term challenger is Beam Therapeutics' **BEAM-101**, a base editing approach (Phase 1/2 BEACON trial). All 26 patients dosed to date achieved foetal haemoglobin levels exceeding 60%, with a BLA filing targeted for late 2026.

> Beam Therapeutics (January 2025); GlobeNewswire

### CMS Outcomes-Based Payment Deal

On 4 December 2024, the US Centers for Medicare and Medicaid Services (CMS) announced both Vertex (Casgevy) and bluebird bio (Lyfgenia) would participate in the **Cell and Gene Therapy (CGT) Access Model** — a first-of-its-kind outcomes-based arrangement under which Vertex must rebate a portion of the payment if Casgevy underperforms expected health outcomes over the follow-up period. 33 states, Washington D.C., and Puerto Rico — representing 84% of Medicaid beneficiaries with SCD — are participating from March 2025.

> CMS (December 2024); AHA News (4 Dec 2024)
